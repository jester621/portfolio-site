June 2025 - Sri Lanka Government Websites DDoS Attack

In June 2025, several Sri Lankan government websites, including the official gov.lk portal and the Central Bank of Sri Lanka site, were targeted by a large-scale Distributed Denial-of-Service (DDoS) attack. Thousands of compromised devices overwhelmed these servers, causing extended downtime and disruption of online public services.

The government collaborated with cybersecurity firms to mitigate the attack by deploying advanced filtering and strengthening infrastructure security.

April 2025 - Ransomware Targeting Local Banks

In April 2025, a ransomware attack encrypted critical servers at major banks such as Sampath Bank and Hatton National Bank (HNB). The attackers demanded payment in cryptocurrency for decryption keys.

The banks promptly isolated affected systems and engaged cybersecurity teams along with local authorities to handle the incident. This event underscored the importance of maintaining secure backups and educating staff on ransomware threats.

March 2025 - Data Leak at Sri Lankan Telecom Provider

A data leak occurred in March 2025 at Dialog Axiata, where a misconfigured database exposed personal subscriber data including phone numbers and billing addresses.

The breach raised awareness about the need for proper data access management and stronger encryption policies across telecom providers.

January 2025 - Phishing Campaign Impersonating Local Banks

Starting January 2025, phishing emails mimicking communications from National Savings Bank (NSB) and Bank of Ceylon (BOC) targeted customers to steal their login credentials and personal information.

Public awareness campaigns by these banks and CERT Sri Lanka educated users on identifying phishing emails and avoiding fraudulent links.
